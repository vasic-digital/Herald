**The spine = HRD-018 (Batch A).** It is foundational because:

1. **HRD-019..025 (flavor bindings)** are concrete `commons_constitution.Evaluator` implementations whose whole purpose is to call the emit helpers (`PolicyViolation`, `GateFailed`, `HostSafetyBreach`, `CredentialLeak`, тАж). They cannot ship usefully until the emit path is durable end-to-end (event тЖТ DB-persisted with bundle-hash). The framework methods exist; the persistence + bundle-hash-on-emit + mode-ladder gating is what makes a binding's PASS real rather than a ┬з107 in-memory bluff.
2. **HRD-026 (bundle-hash captureer)** is the per-event SHA that every emitted event MUST carry for replayability (┬з42.1.3). It is a sub-component of A.
3. **HRD-027 (mode-ladder)** is the allow/warn/enforce gate every binding's `Evaluate` consults. Sub-component of A.
4. **HRD-090 (dead-letter)** emits a `.queue.dead_letter` event via `commons_constitution` per ┬з42.1 тЖТ depends on A's persistence path being live.
5. A subset of ┬з43 catalogue commands emit events (HRD-036 `cherald creds scan` тЖТ `.credential.leak`; HRD-040 `sherald constitution pull` тЖТ `.bundle.updated`; HRD-056 `sherald mem-budget watch` тЖТ `.host.safety_breach`) тЖТ those rows depend on A.

**What A unblocks (highest leverage):** all of Batch B (7 HRDs), HRD-090 in Batch D, and ~4 event-emitting rows in Batch C. That is why A is the single highest-value first unit.

**What does NOT depend on A (parallelisable from day one):** HRD-081 + HRD-085..089 (TaskRepository surfaces that are pure pgx CRUD against the `background` submodule interface), and the non-event-emitting ┬з43 catalogue commands (the doc-sync / export / format wrappers тАФ HRD-037, HRD-038, HRD-039, HRD-048, HRD-050, HRD-052, HRD-065-class).

## ┬з2. Catalogue-check reuse map (wiring vs new code)

Per Universal ┬з11.4.74, HRD-018's catalogue-check (`docs/catalogue-checks/HRD-018-foundation.md`) already established that **9 of 12 capabilities resolve to `extend` against existing Helix-stack submodules** тАФ the "implementation" is WIRING, not writing from scratch. The submodules are already vendored under `submodules/` (auth, background, cache, config, database, eventbus, middleware, observability, recovery тАФ all present, verified). Reuse map:
